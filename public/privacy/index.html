Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect personal information. Effective July 8, 2026. Last updated July 8, 2026.

Red Water LLC, doing business as Redwater Revenue ("Redwater," "we," "us," or "our") provides this Privacy Policy to explain how we handle personal information when you visit redwaterrev.com (the "Site"), contact us, subscribe to our newsletter, book a call, or otherwise interact with us.

Two different roles. Redwater plays two roles with respect to personal information:

  • As a business for information about our own website visitors, leads, newsletter subscribers, and clients. This Policy governs that information.
  • As a service provider for the personal information contained in the custom software, CRMs, and automations we build and host for our clients. We process that information only on our clients' behalf and under their instructions, governed by our Data Processing Addendum with each client, not by this Policy. If you are an end customer of one of our clients and want to exercise privacy rights, please contact that business directly.

1. Information we collect

Information you provide directly:

  • Contact form: your name, email address, company name, industry, and the contents of your message.
  • Newsletter (The Revenue Report): your email address.
  • Booking a call: your name, email address, industry, any notes you choose to add, and scheduling details.
  • Client onboarding and billing: business contact details and information needed to deliver and invoice our services.

Information collected automatically:

  • Server and usage logs: IP address, browser type, device information, pages visited, and timestamps, collected by our hosting provider for security and reliability.

Analytics. We use two analytics tools to understand how the Site is used:

  • Plausible Analytics, a privacy focused, cookieless service that measures aggregate traffic such as page views and referring sites. It does not set cookies, does not collect personal information, and does not track you across other websites.
  • Microsoft Clarity, a product analytics tool that helps us see how visitors use the Site through aggregated metrics, heatmaps, and session replays (recordings of on page actions such as clicks, scrolls, and mouse movement). Clarity uses cookies and similar technologies and may capture usage and device information. Sensitive input fields are masked by default. Microsoft processes this data as our service provider and also for its own purposes under the Microsoft Privacy Statement and the Clarity terms of use.

We do not use advertising pixels (such as the Meta Pixel) to build advertising audiences.

Cookies. Plausible is cookieless. Microsoft Clarity sets cookies to measure and replay site usage. You can control or delete cookies through your browser settings, and blocking them will not affect your ability to use the Site.

Sensitive personal information. We do not intentionally collect sensitive categories of personal information (such as government IDs, precise geolocation, health, biometric, or financial account data) through the Site.

Children. The Site is intended for business audiences and is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us information, contact us and we will delete it.

2. How we use information

We use personal information to respond to inquiries and provide, operate, and improve our services; send our newsletter and business communications you request; schedule and conduct calls and consultations; send administrative, transactional, and billing communications; maintain the security, integrity, and performance of the Site; and comply with legal obligations and enforce our agreements.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use it for our own cross context behavioral advertising. Some analytics providers we use (such as Microsoft Clarity) may process usage data for their own purposes as described in the Analytics section above; you can limit this through your browser cookie controls.

3. How we share information

We share personal information only with service providers who process data on our behalf to run our business (for example, hosting, database, email, scheduling, and payment providers); professional advisors (lawyers, accountants) as needed; legal and safety recipients when required by law or to protect rights, property, or safety; and in a business transfer such as a merger or acquisition, subject to this Policy. A current list of our service providers is at redwaterrev.com/subprocessors. Each is bound by contract to use the data only to provide services to us.

4. The service providers we use (Site)

For our own website and business operations, we use, among others: Supabase (database and storage for form submissions), ConvertKit (newsletter email), Google (calendar scheduling and workspace tools), Plausible Analytics (privacy friendly, cookieless site analytics), Vercel (website hosting), and payment and email providers for client billing. Our full, current list, including the vendors that power the software we build for clients, is at redwaterrev.com/subprocessors.

5. Artificial intelligence

Some of the software and automations we build for clients use third party AI providers (such as OpenAI and Anthropic) to process data on the client's behalf, for example to draft or summarize documents. When we use these providers, they act as our service providers, under their business and API terms they do not train their models on this data, and they are bound by data processing agreements. AI processing of a client's end customer data is disclosed to those end customers by the client and governed by our Data Processing Addendum, not by this Site Policy. For our own Site, we do not feed your contact form or newsletter data into AI systems for any purpose other than delivering the service you requested.

6. Text messaging (SMS)

Some services we operate, including tools we build and run for our clients, send text messages through providers such as Twilio. This section describes how mobile numbers and SMS consent are handled.

  • Consent. We (or our clients) send SMS only to people who have given prior express consent, for example by submitting a mobile number and opting in through a form, checkbox, or written agreement. Consent to receive texts is never a condition of any purchase.
  • Types of messages. Depending on the program, messages may include appointment confirmations and reminders, account and service notifications, and customer service replies, and, only where separately consented to, promotional messages.
  • Message frequency varies by service and by your interactions.
  • Message and data rates may apply.
  • Opt out. Reply STOP to any message to opt out at any time. You may also opt out by any other reasonable method. We honor opt outs promptly.
  • Help. Reply HELP for assistance, or contact us at jack@redwaterrev.com.
  • Mobile opt in data is never shared. No mobile information, including phone numbers and SMS opt in or consent data, will be shared with third parties or affiliates for their own marketing or promotional purposes. We share it only with the messaging providers (such as Twilio) needed to deliver the messages you requested.

7. Your privacy choices and rights

  • Opt out of the newsletter anytime using the unsubscribe link in any email.
  • Access, correct, or delete the personal information we hold about you as a business. Email us at jack@redwaterrev.com and we will respond within 45 days.
  • Do Not Track. Some browsers send a Do Not Track signal. Because there is no common industry standard for these signals, we do not currently respond to Do Not Track signals.

Depending on your state of residence, you may have additional rights (to know, delete, correct, port your data, and to appeal a denial). Most US state privacy laws currently apply to larger businesses, and Redwater is generally below those thresholds, but we honor the access, correction, and deletion requests described above regardless. To make a request, contact jack@redwaterrev.com. We will not discriminate against you for exercising any right.

8. Data retention

We keep personal information only as long as needed for the purposes described here, to respond to you, deliver and bill for services, meet legal and tax obligations, and maintain business records, and then delete or de identify it. Newsletter data is kept until you unsubscribe.

9. Security

We use reasonable administrative and technical safeguards appropriate to the nature of the data, including encrypted transmission (HTTPS), access controls, and reputable infrastructure providers. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, so we cannot guarantee absolute security. If we experience a data breach affecting your personal information, we will notify you and applicable authorities as required by Louisiana law and any other applicable law.

10. Data location

Redwater operates in the United States, and our providers process data in the United States. If you access the Site from outside the US, you understand your information will be processed in the US.

11. Third party links

The Site may link to third party websites. We are not responsible for their privacy practices. Review their policies separately.

12. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. We will post the updated version here with a new "Last updated" date, and for material changes we will provide additional notice where appropriate.
