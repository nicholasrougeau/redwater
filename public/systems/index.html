Systems

Adopting AI, in four shapes.

For most local businesses, adopting AI lands in one of these four buckets. The audit decides which one moves your business first. They are not products off a shelf, they are built to fit how you already work.

01 · Single source of truth

Custom CRMs.

A CRM is the system your business runs its customers and jobs on. Instead of a generic tool you bend to fit, we build the pipeline, the fields, and the stages around how you actually sell and deliver, so everything about a customer lives in one place.

What it usually includes
  • A pipeline with the stages your business really uses
  • One customer record: contact, history, jobs, and notes
  • Estimates, work orders, and invoicing tied to each job
  • The repetitive follow-up automated so nothing slips
See an example →
02 · One pane of glass

Custom dashboards.

A dashboard pulls every number that actually matters into one view that updates on its own. No more exporting spreadsheets and stitching them together the night before a meeting. You open one screen and the picture is already there.

What it usually includes
  • An overview tab: revenue, pipeline, and activity at a glance
  • Live pulls from the tools you already use
  • The few numbers you check every day, front and center
  • Quiet flags when something is worth a second look
See an example →
03 · Quiet background work

Custom automations.

An automation is a piece of software that does a repetitive job for you, in the background, without anyone remembering to start it. We map the bottlenecks costing you the most hours and quietly remove them.

A common one: intake automation

When a new lead comes in, software captures it, fills in the missing details, files it where it belongs, and kicks off the first follow-up, all before anyone touches it. No lead falls through, and your team starts every conversation already informed.

See an example →
04 · Built to fit

Internal tools.

Internal tools are the day-to-day software your team opens to get work done: scheduling, inventory, job tracking, field and inspection apps. When the off-the-shelf option does not fit your workflow, we build the one that does.

A common one: scheduling & dispatch

A tool that puts the right person on the right job at the right time. Jobs, crews, and calendars in one place, so dispatching stops living in someone's head and on a whiteboard.
